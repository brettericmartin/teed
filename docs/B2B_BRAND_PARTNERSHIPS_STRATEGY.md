## 1. Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

The creator economy has reached $33 billion in market value (2025), with brands increasing creator marketing budgets by 171% year-over-year. Yet a critical infrastructure gap persists: **there is no single source of truth for product information that serves both brands AND creators**.

Brands struggle to track how creators use their products. Creators waste hours hunting for accurate product specs, images, and affiliate links. Teed is uniquely positioned to solve both problems by becoming the **canonical product reference layer** between brands and creators.

### The Vision

**Teed Brand Partnerships** transforms Teed from a creator utility into essential B2B infrastructure - the "Shopify of product information" for the creator economy. Brands maintain verified product catalogs on Teed. When creators build bags (gear collections), they pull from these canonical sources, ensuring accuracy while giving brands unprecedented visibility into organic product usage.

### Strategic Fit

This opportunity aligns with Teed's core identity:
- **Canonical reference utility** - Teed already aims to be the authoritative source for gear information
- **Infrastructure, not destination** - Brand catalogs make Teed more valuable as plumbing, not a competing platform
- **Creator empowerment** - Accurate product data and auto-affiliate links increase creator independence

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 The Brand Problem

**Challenge: Invisible Organic Usage**

When creators organically feature products in their content, brands have zero visibility:

- **No tracking**: Brands don't know which creators use their products authentically
- **No attribution**: Organic mentions drive sales but can't be measured
- **No control**: Product information across creator content is often inaccurate or outdated
- **No discovery**: Brands can't find creators who genuinely love their products

**Current Workarounds**

| Solution | Limitation |
|----------|------------|
| **Influencer Marketing Platforms** (GRIN, CreatorIQ) | Expensive ($10K-$30K/year), focused on paid campaigns, not organic usage |
| **Google Alerts** | Manual, misses most content, no structured data |
| **Direct Outreach** | Doesn't scale, misses authentic users |
| **Affiliate Networks** | Only tracks purchases, not product features |

**The Pain Point**

> "We spend $2M annually on influencer campaigns, but our best brand ambassadors are creators who organically use our gear and we don't even know they exist." - Marketing Director, Major Outdoor Brand

### 2.2 The Creator Problem

**Challenge: Product Information Hell**

Creators waste significant time on product data management:

- **Spec hunting**: Hours spent finding accurate product specifications
- **Image sourcing**: Searching for high-quality, approved product photos
- **Link management**: Maintaining affiliate links across multiple posts and platforms
- **Outdated information**: Product names, prices, and availability change constantly
- **Compliance risk**: Using incorrect product claims or unauthorized images

**Current Pain Points**

| Task | Time Wasted Weekly | Creator Frustration |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Finding accurate specs | 2-4 hours | "Every brand website is different" |
| Sourcing approved images | 1-2 hours | "Am I even allowed to use this?" |
| Managing affiliate links | 2-3 hours | "Links break constantly" |
| Verifying product names | 1 hour | "Is it the 'Pro' or 'Pro Plus' model?" |
| Updating old content | 1-2 hours | "I can't update 50 YouTube descriptions" |

**The Pain Point**

> "I spend more time researching product details than actually creating content. Every brand has different specs formats, different affiliate programs, and I never know if I have the current information." - Golf Content Creator

### 2.3 The Market Gap

**No One Owns the Product Information Layer**

Current ecosystem participants each serve partial needs:

| Player | What They Do | What They Miss |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Shopify** | E-commerce infrastructure | Creator content, organic tracking |
| **Affiliate Networks** | Commission tracking | Product information, brand discovery |
| **Influencer Platforms** | Campaign management | Organic usage, canonical data |
| **PIM Systems** (Salsify, Akeneo) | Product data management | Creator interface, usage tracking |

**Teed's Unique Position**

Teed sits at the intersection of:
1. **Product information** - Structured, canonical product data
2. **Creator workflows** - Where creators build and share gear collections
3. **Distribution touchpoints** - Embeds, exports, and shares across platforms

This creates a natural "watering hole" where brands can meet creators organically.

### 4.2 Catalog Management

**Product Data Structure**

Each product in a verified catalog includes:

```typescript
interface VerifiedProduct {
  // Core Identity
  id: string;
  brand_id: string;
  sku: string;
  name: string;
  model_year?: number;

  // Classification
  category: ProductCategory;
  subcategory?: string;
  tags: string[];

  // Details
  description: string;
  features: string[];
  specifications: Record<string, string | number>;

  // Media
  images: ProductImage[];
  videos?: ProductVideo[];

  // Pricing & Availability
  msrp: Money;
  current_price?: Money;
  availability: 'in_stock' | 'limited' | 'out_of_stock' | 'discontinued';

  // Commerce
  purchase_url: string;
  affiliate_links: AffiliateLink[];

  // Metadata
  verified_at: Date;
  last_updated: Date;
}
```

### 5.4 Independence Preservation

**Critical Design Principle**

This feature MUST increase creator independence, not decrease it.

**How We Protect Creator Independence**

1. **No lock-in**: Creators can still add custom products (not just verified)
2. **Portable data**: Export all bag data anytime
3. **Optional participation**: Verified catalogs are optional, not required
4. **Creator-first affiliate**: Creator's affiliate ID takes priority
5. **Privacy by default**: Creators opt-in to brand visibility

### 7.3 Catalog Sync Integrations

**Shopify Integration**

```typescript
// lib/integrations/shopify/ShopifyCatalogSync.ts

interface ShopifySyncConfig {
  shopifyDomain: string;
  accessToken: string;
  brandId: string;
}

class ShopifyCatalogSync {
  async syncProducts(config: ShopifySyncConfig): Promise<SyncResult> {
    // 1. Fetch products from Shopify Admin API
    const products = await this.fetchShopifyProducts(config);

    // 2. Transform to Teed product format
    const teedProducts = products.map(this.transformProduct);

    // 3. Upsert to brand_products table
    const result = await this.upsertProducts(config.brandId, teedProducts);

    return result;
  }

  private transformProduct(shopifyProduct: ShopifyProduct): BrandProduct {
    return {
      external_id: shopifyProduct.id.toString(),
      sku: shopifyProduct.variants[0]?.sku,
      name: shopifyProduct.title,
      description: shopifyProduct.body_html,
      images: shopifyProduct.images.map(img => ({
        url: img.src,
        alt: img.alt,
        primary: img.position === 1
      })),
      msrp: parseFloat(shopifyProduct.variants[0]?.price || '0'),
      // ... additional mapping
    };
  }
}
```

**Analytics Aggregation**

Hourly cron job aggregates usage data:

```sql
-- Update daily brand analytics
INSERT INTO brand_analytics (brand_id, date, products_featured, unique_creators, total_bags)
SELECT
  bp.brand_id,
  CURRENT_DATE,
  COUNT(DISTINCT pu.brand_product_id),
  COUNT(DISTINCT pu.creator_id),
  COUNT(DISTINCT bi.bag_id)
FROM product_usage pu
JOIN brand_products bp ON pu.brand_product_id = bp.id
JOIN bag_items bi ON pu.bag_item_id = bi.id
WHERE pu.added_at >= CURRENT_DATE
GROUP BY bp.brand_id
ON CONFLICT (brand_id, date)
DO UPDATE SET
  products_featured = EXCLUDED.products_featured,
  unique_creators = EXCLUDED.unique_creators,
  total_bags = EXCLUDED.total_bags;
```

## 11. Legal Considerations

### 11.1 FTC Compliance

**Disclosure Requirements**

Per [FTC Guidelines](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers), both brands AND creators are responsible for proper disclosure.

**Teed's Role:**

1. **Automatic disclosure support** - When bags contain affiliate links from brand catalogs, disclosure language auto-appears
2. **Brand responsibility messaging** - Brands agree to FTC compliance in terms
3. **Creator education** - Help resources on disclosure requirements

**Implementation:**

```typescript
// When displaying bags with brand products + affiliate links
const disclosureRequired = bagHasAffiliateLinks(bag);
if (disclosureRequired) {
  displayDisclosure("This collection contains affiliate links. The creator may earn a commission on purchases.");
}
```

### 11.2 Data Privacy

**Creator Data Shared with Brands**

| Data | Shared | Creator Control |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Username/handle | Optional | Opt-in |
| Follower count | Optional | Opt-in |
| Bags/items | If public | Bag privacy setting |
| Contact info | Never | N/A |
| Earnings | Never | N/A |

**Brand Data Protection**

- Brand pricing and product data protected
- Aggregate analytics shared, not raw data
- Competitor brands cannot see each other's creator lists

**GDPR / CCPA Compliance**

- Data processing agreements with brands
- User consent for tracking
- Data deletion upon request
- Clear privacy policy

### 11.3 Terms & Agreements

**Brand Partner Agreement**

Key terms:
- Acceptable use of creator data
- Prohibition on direct scraping
- Affiliate link requirements
- FTC compliance acknowledgment
- Termination and data handling

**Creator Terms Update**

Additions needed:
- Consent to brand visibility (opt-in)
- Affiliate link disclosure requirements
- Data sharing preferences

### 11.4 Intellectual Property

**Brand Content**

- Brands own their product data and images
- License to Teed for platform display
- Creator usage rights defined by brand

**Creator Content**

- Creators own their bags, notes, and custom content
- Brands cannot claim creator content
- Clear attribution requirements
